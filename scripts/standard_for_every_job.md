# **The** **Standard** **for Every Job.**

  

# **Purpose:**

# This playbook defines the required standard for on-site service delivery. It exists to create a consistent, professional, and exceptional customer experience from first contact through departure.

  

# **This is not guidance.**

# **This is the standard.**

  

# *Every teammate is expected to know it, follow it, and execute it on every job.*

  

### Our brand purpose, **making space for possibility**, shapes how we approach every job.  We’re not just removing junk, we’re helping people create fresh starts and new opportunities\!

  

Every customer interaction you have becomes a chance to facilitate someone’s **transformation.**

The 4 Commercial Service Standards |  

These are the four pillars of High Performance. Every action a Truck Team Member takes falls into at least one of these buckets:  
  
EfficiencyMaximizing revenue per hour without sacrificing safety or quality. It is not about rushing; it is about moving with purpose, planning the route, and respecting the customer's time. CommunicationThe ability to convey value to a customer and establish proper, realistic expectations. Nothing is a surprise, and everything feels like it has a purpose. Documentation The operational integrity that protects the franchise. It is the discipline of accurate data: clear photos, detailed notes, and correct payment processing. If it isn't documented, it didn't happen. ProfessionalismThis gives the customer confidence that we are the experts. They trust us to interact with their customers, employees, and co-represent their brand.

Phase 1: The Call Ahead |  
Objective: Set clear expectations, establish professionalism, and begin building trust before arrival. |  
Prohibited Actions |  
|    - Discussing or quoting pricing
  - Providing an exact arrival time unless within 10 minutes
  - Speaking in a monotone, rushed, or transactional manner
  - Over-talking or dominating the conversation
  - Playing loud music or speaking in the background
  - Using slang or casual language that reduces professionalism
  - Responding immediately without allowing a brief pause
  - Failing to pause or talking over the customer
  - Ending the call before the customer does
  - NOT doing the call at all  
Required Actions |  
✅  
- Place the call 30 minutes before arrival
  - Use an upbeat, confident, and professional tone
  - Always start by clearly stating that you are with 1-800-GOT-JUNK?
  - Introduce yourself and your partner clearly 
  - Address the customer using the correct prefix and last name
  - Allow for a brief pause after customer finishes speaking before responding
  - Ask relevant questions to understand the customer’s priorities and end goal
  - Elaborate on specific items we CAN take
  - Provide a 15 minute arrival window 
  - Communicate immediately if arrival time changes 
  - Ask where the customer prefers we park and how they prefer be notified upon arrival
  - End the call with a positive and reassuring phrase    
What is it? |  

PurposeSet expectations, reduce friction, establish professionalism, and build the customer's excitement before arrival. This is Friction Point #1 This call sets the tone for the entire experience. Commercial Service Standards:Communication, Professionalism  
  
Key Differences from ResidentialCSA has made the pre-service call prior to the call aheadReference the updated notes and validate information.  
Execution By Customer Type |  

The Standard:  - Call ahead happens 30 minutes before arrival window begins 
  - Ask relevant questions to understand customers’ priorities and end goal
  - Use a confident, professional tone  

Residential:  - Lead with warmth and energy
  - Build immediate trust and rapport
  - Clarify the project and identify value drivers
  - Reinforce that the customer is our sole focus   

Commercial:  - Demonstrate respect for the business’s time and operation
  - Confirm site access, contact name, and arrival logistics
  - Communicate clearly and calmly - our business is taking care of their business

Phase 2: Truck Arrival |  
Objective: Create a calm, professional, and respectful first impression. |  
Prohibited Actions (When We Arrive) |  
|    - Parking in grass, restricted areas, or blocking driveway
  - Smoking, vaping, spitting, or using profanity
  - Playing loud music upon arrival and within a mile of the property
  - Arriving in a dirty, disorganized truck
  - Sitting in the truck after parking  
Required Actions (When We Arrive) |  
✅ |    - Approach the location slowly and deliberately
  - No smoking in trucks, including vaping
  - Turn off the radio
  - Use a spotter at all times when backing up or parking
  - Park on the street unless otherwise directed by the customer
  - Ensure the truck and team present a clean, professional appearance  
Prohibited Actions (Truck Appearance) |  
|    - Dirty trucks
  - Wheels with dirt and sludge
  - Missing decals or visible scratches
  - Broken or ripped tarps
  - Cracked windshield
  - Foggy or dirty windows
  - Dashboard full of trash and supplies    
Required Actions (Truck Appearance) |  
✅ |    - Clean, shiny trucks
  - Wheels clean and free of dirt and debris
  - All decals intact and visible
  - Tarp functional with no tears
  - Windshields crack-free
  - Windows clean inside and out
  - Dashboard free of all trash and supplies
  - No trash in the cab  
Prohibited Actions (Uniform Standards) |  
|    - Untucked shirt
  - Any pants or shorts that are not navy blue
  - Wrinkled or dirty uniform (Grease or Paint)
  - Hat backwards
  - Black boots or tennis shoes
  - Any color belt that is not brown
  - Messy hair or ungroomed facial hair
  - Body odor (No smoke, vape or locker room odor)             
Required Actions (Uniform Standards) |  
✅ |    - Shirt tucked in
  - Navy blue pants or shorts
  - Shirt and pants wrinkle-free
  - Hat worn forward and straight
  - Brown belt
  - Brown boots fully laced
  - Clean and professional hygiene   
What is it? |  
|  PurposeDeliver on the first promise made to the customer. The truck and appearance represent the brand before a word is spoken. Commercial Service Standards:Efficiency, Professionalism  
Key Differences from ResidentialAvoid blocking customer/employee traffic flow. Be aware of fire lanes, handicap parking, and low clearance entries, exits, and awnings.  
Execution By Customer Type |  

The Standard:  - Arrive as communicated
  - Park exactly where instructed
  - Present a clean, professional appearance
  - No distractions, no disruptions  

Residential:  - Show confidence and positive energy
  - Make the customer feel prioritized
  - Reinforce trust through body language and presence  

Commercial:  - Fit into the customer’s operation
  - Respect loading zones, docks, and designated areas (fire lanes, handicap parking, etc.)
  - Move with purpose and professionalism

Phase 3: Walk-up and Greeting |  
Objective: Establish trust and professionalism within the first moments of face-to-face interaction. |  
Prohibited Actions (Walk Up) |  
|    - Walking through landscaping or grass
  - Aggressive, excessive, or careless knocking or ringing of the doorbell
  - Standing too close to the door or customer
  - Looking through windows or around the property
  - Entering the home without invitation  
Required Actions (Walk Up) |  
✅ |    - Walk directly from the truck to the front door using clear, respectful paths with partner present 
  - Knock directly on the hinge side of the door unless instructed otherwise
  - Take 2 steps back after knocking 
  - Stand confidently with open, neutral body language  
Prohibited Actions (Greeting) |  
|    - Casual or distracted introduction
  - Using slang when addressing the customer 
  - Commenting on the home with negative judgment 
  - Commenting on customers’ belongings with negative judgment 
  - Smelling like smoke        
Required Actions  (Greeting) |  
✅ |    - Introduce yourself and your partner
  - Address the customer using the correct prefix and last name 
  - Use the Dream Big standard greeting (the ‘5 Star Agenda’ presentation)
  - Communicate appreciation and willingness to help
  - Include phrases such as “Happy to help”  
What is it? |  

PurposeEstablish confidence before any work begins. Respect creates trust. Trust creates permission. Commercial Service Standards:Communication, Professionalism Key Differences from ResidentialConnect a consistent message from the pre-service call, call ahead, and introduction (continuity). We aren’t always dealing with the decision maker so we validate who owns the removal before proceeding.           
Execution By Customer Type |  

The Standard:  - Crew approaches together
  - Professional greeting using customer’s name
  - Confirm correct on-site contact  

Residential:  - Friendly, human connection
  - Warm greeting that sets emotional comfort
  - Create permission for collaboration  
| Commercial:  - Direct, professional introduction
  - Confirm who owns the removal on the customer side
  - Create consistency by echoing the message from the CSA notes

Phase 4: Initial Walk-Through |  
Objective: Fully understand the customer’s needs, goals, and priorities before pricing. |  
Prohibited Actions |  
|    - Itemizing or discussing pricing during the walk-through
  - Expressing judgment or personal opinions 
  - Loading items prior to estimate approval 
  - Being distracted (phone or side conversations)
  - Don’t rush the customer 
  - Speaking over the customer   
Required Actions |  
✅ |    - Walk through every applicable space with the customer 
  - Ask thoughtful questions about the project/goals and actively listen
  - Express empathy (the ability to understand and share the feelings of another) when necessary
  - Clarify the customers’ end goal and intended use of the space 
  - Explore other areas of property by offering solutions to their projects/goals
  - Take time to ask about additional items
  - Identify customer pain points and value keys
  - Reinforce that we are full service when relevant
  - Confirm the full scope out loud (“So today we’re removing X, Y, and Z—did I miss anything?”) 
  - Engage your partner so the conversation feels balanced and natural
  - Stay present with the customer 
  - Inspect through piles (with permission) or touch items when appropriate
  - Flag safety concerns (sharp items, biohazards, heavy objects)
  - Identify access limitations (stairs, tight hallways, elevators, HOA rules)
  - Reference similar projects we’ve completed and assisted with challenges very similar
  - Confirm who the decision-maker is if multiple people are present  
What is it? |  

PurposeCreate a shared understanding of scope and expectations. We do not price items. We solve problems.  
Commercial Service Standards:Efficiency, Communication, Documentation, Professionalism  
  
Key Differences from ResidentialWe take diligent notes for documentation as the areas are generally larger and more complex. Use the business’s prior vendor experiences to inform positive and negative service bias.  
Execution By Customer Type |  

The Standard:  - Walk all applicable areas
  - Ask clarifying questions
  - Identify customer priorities
  - No pricing discussion  

Residential:  - Listen actively and build emotional trust
  - Identify pain points and desired outcomes
  - Reinforce full-service capabilities  

Commercial:  - Confirm job notes, access paths, and constraints
  - Identify elevators, docks, staging, security
  - Take visible notes and photos with permission

Phase 5: The Estimate |  
Objective: Present pricing clearly, confidently, and professionally. |  
Prohibited Actions |  
|    - Starting with recycling fees
  - Explaining fees, items, or scenarios that don’t apply 
  - Handing clipboard to customer
  - Using filler words or unconfident language
  - Giving a single price point unless it’s a 1/6th or less
  - Undermining partners pricing or presentation
  - Sales swear word - Is the price okay
  - Starting with minimum pricing
  - Say “unfortunately” or “worst case scenario”
  - Wasting the customers time (keep it relevant)  
Required Actions |  
✅ |    - Present the estimate with partner present
  - Hold the clipboard and confidently reference the price sheet
  - Explain full scope of the service we provide 
  - Be specific and relevant to project
  - Clearly explain truck volume and what the price represents
  - Start with full load and work your way down 
  - Deliver pricing with clarity, confidence, and enthusiasm 
  - Always use the rules of the range (see CSL training)
  - Use assumptive close aligned with customer value keys 
  - Identify and adjust to customer personality type       
What is it? |  

PurposeBuild trust through clarity and accuracy. Clarity builds confidence. Confidence builds trust. Commercial Service Standards:Efficiency, Communication, Documentation, Professionalism Key Differences from ResidentialOftentimes there is an official bidding process, and we will not have the opportunity to complete the removal same-day. Commercial customers expect heavy documentation and itemization.  
Execution By Customer Type |  

The Standard:  - Explain scope before price
  - Present pricing confidently
  - Follow approval rules without exception  

Residential:  - Frame pricing around value and relief
  - Use assumptive close aligned to customer’s priorities
  - Maintain positive, confident delivery        

Commercial:  - Explain pricing clearly 
  - Follow the requirements:
      
      - Follow the 2+ truck rule. GM/CSA approval required before sharing price
      - Use the ‘Commercial Bidding Document’ on every estimate to give CSA information

Phase 6: Preparation, Removal, and Truck Loading |  
Objective: Prepare the site and equipment for safe, efficient, and professional execution. The goal is to execute the job efficiently while demonstrating care, respect, and professionalism. |  
Prohibited Actions |  
|    - Throwing items into the truck 
  - Removing items not included in the approved estimate 
  - Dragging items against unprotected flooring 
  - Leaving entry and exits blocked 
  - Beginning work with disorganized truck 
  - Leaving the truck running unattended
  - Parking illegally or in a way that risks tickets or complaints 
  - Burying metal or donatable items  
Required Actions |  
✅ |    - Proper PPE and relevant tools
  - Confirm which doors may remain open
  - Ensure the removal path is clear
  - Stage the truck for the customer
  - Truck doors open, current contents stacked and organized
  - Explain truck space and volume clearly
  - Load items properly and intentionally
  - Standard customer-facing explanation of truck loading          ### Standard Truck Loading Procedure1\.  Trash goes on the truck first and is loaded in the back (closest to the cab)
2.  Donations go on second - we don't want to bury, lose, or damage these items
3.  Metal or anything that can be recycled is loaded on last - ensures recycling actually happens and minimizes risk of injury to truck team members   - Assume the customer is observing everything, even when they aren’t present
  - Lay floor protection when needed
  - Protect door frames, corners, and tight turns
  - Identify high-risk obstacles before beginning the removal
  - Start with biggest/most difficult to remove items first (team lifts)  
What is it? |  
|  PurposePrepare deliberately and execute cleanly. This is Friction Point #2 Preparation prevents problems and defects.  How the work is done matters as much as the result. Commercial Service Standards:Efficiency, Communication, Documentation, Professionalism Key Differences from ResidentialWe want to disrupt the work day for employees and customers as little as possible. We educate the customer on the optimal removal path as experts.     
Execution By Customer Type |  

The Standard:  - Protect the space
  - Follow loading procedures
  - Communicate progress throughout  

Residential:  - Protect floors, walls, and personal items
  - Demonstrate visible care for belongings
  - Maintain positive momentum  

Commercial:  - Walk and confirm optimal removal paths
  - Stage efficiently to minimize disruption to the business or their customers
  - Assign roles and execute with precision - who is doing what during the removal?

Phase 7: Cleanup |  
Objective: Leave the space visibly better than it was found and eliminate any trace of disruption caused by the job. |  
Prohibited Actions |  
|    - Leaving debris, dust or residue behind 
  - Rushing cleanup to save time 
  - Ignoring corners, edges, or less visible areas
  - Stuffing items or misc trash into “can’t see areas”
  - Assuming the customer “won’t notice” small messes  
Required Actions |  
✅ |    - Sweep all affected areas and remove all debris, dust, and fragments from the removal
  - Wipe down surfaces or flooring if residue or marks are present
  - Return any moved items to their original or agreed-upon location
  - Straighten and align the space to create a clear visual reset
  - Perform a deliberate final scan to ensure the area looks clean and orderly before inviting the customer back            
What is it? |  

PurposeLeave the space better than found. The job isn’t finished once the last item is loaded.  It is finished when the space feels clean, orderly, and reset. Commercial Service Standards:Efficiency, Professionalism Key Differences from ResidentialWe validate with the business before we move any large items that may be expensive, delicate, or have power connected to them. Returning the business as quickly as possible to normal working conditions is key.  
Execution By Customer Type |  

The Standard:  - Sweep all affected areas
  - Remove debris and dust
  - Reset the space visually  

Residential:  - Restore emotional comfort in the space
  - Ensure the area feels “finished”
  - Eliminate any trace of disruption  

Commercial:  - Return the space to operational readiness - leave the area better than we found it
  - Remove all job-related items from the customer site
  - Respect the business environment - minimal interruption to their day

Phase 8: Final Walk-Through |  
Objective: Confirm completion, reinforce value, and ensure full customer satisfaction. |  
Prohibited Actions |  
|    - Skipping the walk-through
  - Not addressing customers’ concerns and or feedback
  - Being distracted (phone, side conversations)
  - Standing in one place and summarizing rather than walking with customer
  - Rushing the customer through the walk-through
  - Assuming satisfaction without verbal confirmation
  - Pitching reviews before satisfaction is achieved and confirmed
  - Going directly into the payment process  
Required Actions (Framing and Work Validation) |  
✅ |    - Invite the customer to walk the completed space(s) together at customers’ pace
  - Frame the purpose of the final walk-through
      
      - “This is our chance to make sure everything we agreed on is done exactly how you want it.”
  - Walk the entire job area by area, continuing to paint the picture of the completed project
  - Use ownership language (“We removed… We cleared… We took care of…”)
  - Pause after each area to allow questions or feedback, address immediately  
Required Actions (Confirm Satisfaction) |  
✅ |    - Ask clear satisfaction questions before finishing the walk-through 
      
      - “How did everything go from your perspective today?”
      - “Would there be anything else we can do to help in this area?”
  - Do not proceed until satisfaction is over 100%  
Required Actions (Closing the Walk-through) |  
✅ |    - Final Walk-through ends at the truck with open doors - show the customer the results
  - Offer additional capacity (truck space) after satisfaction is confirmed - Costco effect
      
      - Messaged as optional and helpful, not transactional
      - Ex: “You’ve still got ____ space left on the truck if there’s anything else you’d like help with—we would be happy to help”
  - Ask if we can assist them with any other tasks they need an extra pair of hands for (moving furniture, sweeping/vacuuming/mopping, loading items in vehicles, etc.)
  - Deliver the Google review request only after 100% satisfaction is achieved  
What is it? |  

PurposeConfirm completion and satisfaction. Completion is not declared by the crew, it is declared by the customer. Commercial Service Standards:Efficiency, Communication, Documentation, Professionalism  
  
Key Differences from ResidentialBusinesses have large areas, can be spread out, and have complex entry/exit points - leverage the contact for mappingDo not defer to the CSA or “pass the buck” on questions and concerns       
Execution By Customer Type |  

The Standard:  - Walk the space with the customer
  - Review what was completed
  - Address concerns immediately  

Residential:  - Reinforce relief and transformation
  - Confirm satisfaction verbally
  - Create space for appreciation and trust  

Commercial:  - Validate removal completion area-by-area
  - Confirm alignment with documented expectations - did we do what we said we would do?
  - Close the loop professionally by addressing all questions with the customer

Phase 9: Before and After Photos |  
Objective: Create an undeniable proof of work, reinforce value for the customer, and protect the business through clear documentation. |  
Prohibited Actions |  
|    - Low quality, blurry, dark, or unclear images
  - Uploading same photo twice and reusing photos
  - Treating photos as optional
  - Uploading photos that don’t clearly show the work completed
  - No obscene gestures 
  - Do not capture them on personal devices. Dream Big devices only
  - Do not use social media with addresses, faces, or anything that exploits the customers identity  
Required Actions |  
✅ |   - Capture clear before and after photos of all serviced areas
  - Ensure photos clearly show the transformation of the space reclaimed
  - Take photos from consistent angles to clearly demonstrate change
  - Take photos of surcharge (pathway for additional labor, etc.)
  - Ensure truck doors are open when taking a photo
  - If the job exceeds two trucks, clearly indicate which truck is shown using fingers in the photo
  - Ensure photos are well lit, in focus, and unobstructed
  - Include wide shots that show the overall impact, not just close ups
  - Use 0.5 magnification (longest zoom option)
  - Verify photos are saved and attached correctly on authorized work phones
  - Any locations that can’t be walked through must be reviewed with photos
  - Upload all photos to the correct opportunity before leaving the job site
  - Offer to show the customer the before and after transformation
  - Send photos to the customer if they are not on site
  - Ask if they would like to include any photos on their personal social media with their 5 star review
  - Upload picture of donations dropped and donation receipt if possible 
What is it? |  

PurposeCreate proof of work and protect the business. If it isn’t documented, it didn’t happen.  Photos protect the team, prove the value, and tell the story of the work.  
  Commercial Service Standards:Documentation, Professionalism  
  
Key Differences from ResidentialThe contact is not always on site for the removal so proper documentation is key. Businesses have vendors, shareholders, partners, and customers to answer to on how they spend their money and resources.  
Execution By Customer Type |  

The Standard:  - Capture clear before and after photos
  - Upload promptly and accurately  

Residential:  - Reinforce value visually
  - Offer to show transformation
  - Support reviews and trust  

Commercial:  - Before/After of area and trucks
  - Provide clarity and confidence to the customer
  - Ensure clean handoff across teams - this creates continuity for the customer interactions

Phase 10: Pricing and Payment |  
Objective: Close the job with clarity, confidence, and professionalism. |  
Prohibited Actions |  
|    - Apologizing for the price or sounding unsure 
  - Justifying or over explaining the price 
  - Changing the price without a clear, professional explanation 
  - Creating awkward silence through nervous filler words 
  - Rushing the customer through payment 
  - Treating payment as transactional or impersonal 
  - Forcing or pressuring the customer to leave a tip or hovering over them
  - Forcing or pressuring the customer to leave a review or hovering over them
  - Trading a discount for a tip or reviews  
Required Actions |  
✅ |    - Review before and after photos with the client, highlighting the space transformation
  - Clearly state the final price and show them on the payment device how it breaks down using calm, confident language 
  - Answer pricing or payment questions clearly and directly
  - Hand the payment device to the customer with the final price breakdown still displayed (Before charging the card)
  - Use the approved tip screen script exactly as trained 
  - Give the customer space as they are filling out the tip screen
  - Complete payment without rushing or distraction 
  - Use the approved NPS explanation script exactly as trained
  - Leave the customer while they fill out the NPS.      
Approved Tip Screen Script |  
|  "Once you hit that green Done button at the bottom it will move you over to our completely optional gratuity screen.  We never expect anything, which is why you will see a no tip option.  Once you have made your selection, you can go ahead and tap or insert your card to complete payment."  
Approved (pre) NPS Script |  
|  "We hope that we exceeded your expectations today.  We do have a little bit of extra time, is there anything else we can do while we are here to ensure we provide you with a world class experience?"  - Take the time now to take care of any action the customer has requested  
Approved NPS Script |  

"We really appreciate you giving us the opportunity to serve you today. You will receive an email with your receipt, then another from customer experience that asks: “How likely are you to recommend 1-800-GOT-JUNK? to a friend or family member.” We hope that we have provided a service that you would feel great about recommending us to anyone you know. Filling out that quick survey really helps us ensure we are exceeding expectations every time. Once completed you will receive a coupon for the next time you have us out. That coupon is fully transferable and never expires. We would really appreciate it if you took the time to do so while we close up the truck and head out of here. Thank you again\!"
